About

Howdy, my name is Abby! I'm a Wildlife Biologist and Data Analyst located near Washington, DC. I work for the US Fish and Wildlife Service to process and manage migratory bird data with R and Python.

Born and raised in Delaware, I went to college in my hometown of Newark at the University of Delaware to study Wildlife Conservation and Ecology. During and after undergrad I worked on seasonal field jobs from Rhode Island to Florida with species such as Wood Thrush, Piping Plovers, and Red-cockaded Woodpeckers. I eventually settled in Virginia to start graduate school with the Bulluck Avian Ecology Lab at Virginia Commonwealth University, where I studied Red-headed Woodpecker sex-specific parental care behavior and home range size for my MSc. I learned about data wrangling at the Smithsonian Migratory Bird Center, and realized managing wildlife data was the career for me.

I am a certified Associate Wildlife Biologist and happy that my background in ornithology has brought me to the world of data management. In the coming years, I look forward to learning more about data visualization, science communication, and new coding languages.

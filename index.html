About

Howdy, my name is Abby! I'm a Wildlife Biologist and Data Analyst living near Washington, DC. I am currently pursuing my dream job of processing and managing migratory bird data. I work in R and python most days, but my background is in ornithology.

Before starting my current position, I studied Red-headed Woodpecker parental care behavior and home range size for my Master's degree with the Bulluck Avian Ecology Lab at Virginia Commonwealth University. I went to undergrad in my hometown at the University of Delaware, during and after which time I also did a lot of avian field jobs. I worked specifically with Piping Plovers, Roseate Terns, Red-cockaded Woodpeckers, Gray Catbirds, and Wood Thrush, plus a multitude of eastern birds of North America via mist netting and point counts. Over time, my career interests have brought me to the world of data management. In the coming years, I look forward to learning more about the art of data visualization, science communication, and new coding languages.

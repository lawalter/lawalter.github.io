About

Born and raised in Delaware, I attended the University of Delaware to study Wildlife Conservation and Ecology in my hometown of Newark. During and after earning my undergraduate degree, I took seasonal field jobs from Rhode Island to Florida to work with species such as Wood Thrush, Piping Plovers, Red-cockaded Woodpeckers, white-footed deer mice, and frosted flatwoods salamanders. I eventually settled in Virginia to join the Bulluck Avian Ecology Lab at Virginia Commonwealth University, where I studied Red-headed Woodpecker sex-specific parental care behavior and home range size for my Master’s research. I later honed my data wrangling techniques at the Smithsonian Migratory Bird Center and realized managing wildlife data was the career for me.

I am a certified Associate Wildlife Biologist and enjoy birding in my free time.
